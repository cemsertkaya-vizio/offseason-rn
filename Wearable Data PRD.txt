Wearable Data PRD (v2)
Background
Offseason’s vision is to take a user’s wearable data as an input to plan and optimize their ideal wellness routine.
Our Emerging Principal: It’s important for us not to re-invent the data wheel, but build the car.
We will be using the Terra API to push wearable data into our app continuously, when it is available. A user will be able to see their wearable data in Offseason, and how it alters their routine. Terra API gives us historical data (even before a user signs up for Offseason), so we can bring in as much historical context as possible.

	With a low HRV (stressed), a normal resting heart rate, and low sleep consistency (unrested) one can assume the body is operating fine, but is showing signs of stress or overtraining. High intensity workouts with not enough recovery (poor sleep/ nutrition). 

Follicular phase is associated with higher levels in strength, power, and energy due to decreased levels of progesterone and increased levels of estrogen. This is likely more about the lifter's emotional response to hormonal changes, rather than genuine neurological and physiological changes. This is an optimal time for rest, or to induce high levels of recovery, depending on contextual factors surrounding users plan and previous training weeks.
	I would emphasize recovery within the next few days, with the option to still train hard on the day of reading data. RHR is still in a normal range which means the cardiovascular system is still working efficiently, Low HRV indicates poor recovery.

Users in the follicular phase would typically feel more energy - making this an ideal time for HIIT, Strength training, or higher intensity workouts. It is also a great time to promote recovery sessions (the “locked in training”)

	27 different permutations of 3 categories of 3 different data points
Many of the permutations are redundant and will elicit the same answer or response
Similar to our Archetypes for training, we can create answers that encompass many similar scenarios with nominal differences
Green Light Scenarios
Normal or High HRV / Normal or Low RHR
Meaning: These are redundant because they all signal that the Parasympathetic nervous system (rest and digest) is either dominant or well-balanced.
Action: Whether Sleep is High, Normal, or Low on a single day in this zone, the coaching action remains "Proceed with Plan." If Sleep is Low, it's a warning to watch tomorrow's data, but today is a "Go.”
Sympathetic Stress Scenarios
Low HRV / Low RHR / Low, normal, or high sleep
Meaning: interesting situation, low HRV is usually paired with High RHR. Client is either a.) very fit and well rested, low stress and doing well or b.) so overtrained that they are signaling poor recovery (the more common outcome)
Action: Ask the client if they are showing symptoms of fatigue or overtraining, recommend that client focus on activities to reduce sympathetic stress (breathwork, prioritize sleep, limit caffeine consumption if any, nature walks, etc.)  
Acute Stress Scenarios
Low HRV / Normal RHR / Any Sleep
Meaning: Likely caused by an intense workout session, double down on nutrition, consistent quality sleep, hydration, and monitor over the next few days.

Action:
Red Zone Scenarios
Low HRV / High RHR / Any sleep
Meaning: Textbook expression of poor response to stress or a signal of accumulated fatigue. Could be illness, overtraining, mental stress, poor sleep, etc.

Action: Immediate training deloads or rest
Recovery Delays
High HRV / High RHR / Any sleep
Meaning: Typically happens after intense HIIT sessions or Poor sleep where the heart is working hard and the nervous system is trying to bounce back

Action: A more technical recovery day, not broken but not primed (Foam rolling, sauna, active recovery, lighter work, alleviate soreness without further strain, promote bloodflow/ recovery, purposeful gentle movement, yoga, stretching,
